01 / HISTORY

A Legacy Born
in 1955

Notre Dame Science Club was established in 1955 within the walls of Notre Dame College, Dhaka — making it the first college-level science club in the Indian Subcontinent. What began as a small gathering of curious minds has grown into one of the most respected scientific communities in Bangladesh.

For over seven decades, NDSC has nurtured generations of scientists, engineers, doctors, and innovators. With a proud motto of "Science in Human Welfare," the club has remained committed to advancing scientific thought and cultivating intellectual growth among the youth of Bangladesh.

From its humble beginnings, NDSC has evolved into a nationally recognized institution that hosts olympiads, research wings, science festivals, and international-level competitions — shaping Bangladesh's scientific future one student at a time.

OUR GUIDING PRINCIPLE

"Science in
Human Welfare"

Since 1955, this timeless motto has guided every initiative, event, and member of Notre Dame Science Club. We believe that science is not merely an academic pursuit — it is a tool for uplifting humanity, solving real-world problems, and building a better tomorrow. Every experiment, every competition, every discovery at NDSC is made in the spirit of serving the greater good.

02 / MISSION & VISION

Where We Stand &
Where We're Going


🎯
OUR MISSION

Notre Dame Science Club is dedicated to inspiring scientific curiosity and nurturing the next generation of innovators, researchers, and leaders in Bangladesh.

  • Promote science literacy and critical thinking among students
  • Organize olympiads, workshops, and research activities
  • Bridge the gap between academic science and real-world application
  • Foster a culture of inquiry, collaboration, and innovation
🔭
OUR VISION

We envision a Bangladesh where science is a cornerstone of human progress — where every young mind has the opportunity to explore, discover, and contribute to the world.

  • Become a nationally leading platform for youth scientific excellence
  • Represent Bangladesh on international scientific stages
  • Build a lifelong community of science advocates and professionals
  • Use science as a force for social good and sustainable development
